Summary I am a physical oceanographer at NASA Jet Propulsion Laboratory. My research interests include geophysical fluid dynamics, satellite oceanography, mesoscale and submesoscale ocean dynamics, and air-sea interaction. I use theory, numerical simulations and observations to answer basic questions either in ocean physics or their observability. Lately, I have also been investing time in the application of machine learning in oceanography.

  • 2011.06 - 2012.12
    postdoctoral scholar
    Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution, Falmouth, MA
    When the SQG application was in the spotlight, I worked with Glenn Flierl, Amala Mahadevan, and Joe LaCasce on a project that was later published as the interior+surface Quasi-geostrophic (isQG) theory. The significance of this study lies in the recognition of the two surface boundary conditions that can utilize sea surface height and sea surface temperature. This further expands the framework to accept two input surface fields, going beyond the SQG theory, which only takes one input surface field. I also developed the Lagrangian tracking code for the PSOM model, which was later used in Omand et al. (2015, 10.1126/science.126006). The code eventually evolved into the open-source software Octopus and was used in MITgcm simulations.
